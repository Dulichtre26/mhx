About Independence Palace

Independence Palace, also known as Reunification Palace, is a historic landmark in Ho Chi Minh City. Built in 1966, it served as the presidential palace of South Vietnam until 1975. The palace is famous for its role in the Vietnam War and its unique mid-century modern architecture. Visitors can explore its grand rooms, war command center, and historic artifacts.
